Movement Paintings
by Michelle Jader

Michelle Jader did not start her career as an artist. For nearly 20 years Michelle worked in the corporate world crafting marketing companies. For 10 of those years, she owned her own marketing consulting business and had the fortune to partner with several Fortune 500 companies. Michelle loved being a marketing consultant and enjoyed the challenges and the rewards of the business world. However, she began to realize that the moments she felt pure join in her work was when she collaborate with the talented and creative artists of her advertising and design agencies. They sparked something within her. It was the spark that ignited a fire inside of her and drove her to do what she truly loved.

Michelle Jader's work explores moments when we willingly and unwillingly dive into the next phase of our life. Whether it's moving to a new city, starting or ending a n relationship, having a baby, or quitting a job, these moments of endings and beginnings are alternately terrifying and exhilirating. Ready or not, we jump, fall or are pushed and our lives change.

Transitions like these include the sense of falling, lack of control, and the feeling that anything is possible. We're vulnerable in these moments and despite our best efforts, our actions seem to be more public than other times in our lives. To capture these feelings, she paints a series of images on semi-transparent, acrylic panels. The push and pull inherent in having different layers of images support the

work's theme and provide an exciting way to paint motion, change, and emotional release. Her life has had its fair share of change. She owned her own marketing conuslting business for ten years and worked with Fortune 500 companies before following her heart and earning an MFA in fine art painting from the Academy of Art University in San Francisco. Oringally from Minnesota, she lives in San Francisco and works out of her studio in the Mission district.

For her it was terrifying, yet an exhilarating time of her life. She felt like she was on the edge of a steep cliff, with no view of the ground floor and only her choice to jump, fall or be pushed. These critical moments are what defined her move into the art world.

As her career progressed, she secretly and increasingly imagined a life where she could spend my time creating art that wasn't subject to unanimous team approval or validated by the number of units moved. She just wanted to create my own art. Finally, in the summer of 2007, when the urge to be a full time artist was too strong. She took the leap and enrolled in San Francisco's Academy of Art University graduate program for fine art painting.
